Privacy Policy

My goal is to make guitab as ethical and human-friendly as possible. As such I will do my best to make sure that your data only goes to people you intend it to go to, and that I don't use your data for anything you don't want me to.

The first and strongest defense of your data is that I don't collect much of it in the first place. I don't track you on this site or any others, and I don't include ads or trackers in our pages. I will never require you to sign up to view the content of this site. I will never require more than a username and password to sign up for the site and contribute content. Providing your name or an email address is optional.

I will never use your data for anything beyond the functionality of the site. Your name will be displayed publicly in your profile if you choose to provide it, so that you can receive credit for your contributions. Your email address will be used to recover access to your account if you forget your password. I won't use either of these things for anything else without asking you first. I may add more mailing features to guitab in the future, but I will never subscribe you to any mail unless you explicitly ask to be subscribed.

Donation pages use Stripe. I don't control what information they collect about you, and I don't store it. See their privacy policy to find out what information they collect. If you would like to make a donation via a different method which fits your privacy needs better, contact me and we can probably work something out.

I promise to do my best to protect what little sensitive data I actually do collect and store from hackers. Passwords are stored using PBKDF2. Requests are served over TLS. In general I'll try to keep up with modern security practices and implement them appropriately on the site.

I promise to do make a reasonable effort to protect your data from illegal government seizures of data. Data is stored in the US and is subject to US law. I won't turn any data over to the government without a warrant. I reserve the right to decide whether I go to court to defend your data if someone comes to me with a warrant. In general, I'll fight any attempt to collect the data of all guitab users, but if law enforcement comes to me with a warrant for the data of a specific user who is accused of a crime, I will provide them that user's data without argument. I will do my best to keep users informed of any warrants I am served with relation to guitab user data.

I will comply with mandatory reporting guidelines for the US and my local laws. I will not go to jail or risk my life to protect your data. I believe privacy is a human right, but the primary functionality of this site is creating guitar tabs and posting them publicly--I'm not running a high-security communications service. Please act accordingly.
